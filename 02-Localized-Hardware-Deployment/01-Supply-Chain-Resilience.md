# Supply Chain Resilience in Domestic Tech

**Status:** Draft / Outline

## The Core Premise
A nation or enterprise that relies entirely on imported, pre-assembled hardware operates with a massive, unpatchable vulnerability. If a device is compromised at the firmware or component level before it even crosses the border, all subsequent software security measures are rendered obsolete.

## Key Arguments to Develop:
1. **The "Black Box" Problem:** The inherent risks of deploying proprietary, imported hardware where component-level auditing is impossible.
2. **Component Counterfeiting & Implants:** How malicious actors intercept supply chains to introduce rogue chips or altered firmware into standard tech equipment before delivery.
3. **The Case for Localized Assembly:** Why shifting toward domestic assembly and modular hardware design is not just an economic strategy, but a critical cybersecurity imperative. Reducing the physical distance between manufacturing and deployment drastically reduces the attack surface.

## Conclusion
Strategic autonomy in technology is impossible without supply chain resilience. Hardware must be audited, understood, and ideally assembled closer to home to establish true trust.
